The Gates Foundation has generally been regarded as well-managed and even received an endorsement from Warren Buffett until recently. However, over time, he too has felt that it has become overly bureaucratic. Despite this, the foundation has made significant contributions to tropical disease research, raised awareness, and developed strategies to mitigate their impact, especially in countries where help is needed most.

The 25-year-old Gates Foundation has already spent more than $100 billion on a range of causes centered mostly around health, education, global development, and gender equality.

Many of the Gates Foundation’s beneficiaries have been people in low-income countries with endemic and frequently deadly diseases like malaria.
